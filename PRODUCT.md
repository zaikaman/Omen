# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Omen is primarily evaluated by hackathon judges who need to understand, in a short live review, whether the system is real, technically deep, and useful. They are looking for evidence that separate agents coordinate through AXL, produce market intelligence through a working workflow, and leave behind verifiable 0G proof artifacts rather than a scripted demo.

Secondary users include protocol sponsors, agent builders, and crypto-native operators who want to inspect how the swarm works, what each role contributed, and whether published signals or intel can be traced back to concrete runtime evidence.

## Product Purpose

Omen is an autonomous market-intelligence swarm that scans market, social, and on-chain context; routes work across specialized agents; generates signals and long-form intel; publishes approved outputs; and records proof trails through 0G and AXL infrastructure.

Success means a judge can quickly see three things: Omen has deep AXL integration across distinct nodes, a strong 0G proof story for runs and artifacts, and a useful market-intelligence workflow that could matter beyond the hackathon.

## Brand Personality

Omen should feel autonomous, precise, and credible. The interface should project independent machine coordination and forensic clarity without sliding into spectacle. It should make technical depth legible through live state, evidence, topology, and traceability.

## Anti-references

Omen must not feel like a generic crypto terminal, flashy AI SaaS, military command center, toy demo, trading-bot scam, or any design that hides weak functionality behind theatrical visuals. Avoid empty alpha claims, fake-looking dashboards, decorative cyberpunk excess, over-polished marketing tropes, and mock data patterns that undermine trust.

## Design Principles

1. Prove the system is real: favor live status, peer IDs, route receipts, artifacts, timestamps, and inspectable run evidence over abstract claims.
2. Make autonomy observable: show how agents coordinate, disagree, finalize, and publish so the swarm feels like a working system, not a static dashboard.
3. Lead with judge comprehension: prioritize fast recognition of AXL depth, 0G proofs, and market-intel utility within the first minute of use.
4. Keep expert density readable: present technical detail in structured layers so dense evidence remains scannable under demo pressure.
5. Treat trust as the product surface: every signal, article, and proof should feel traceable to real execution and infrastructure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline for contrast and interactive states. Do not rely on color alone for status, risk, success, failure, or proof completion. Support reduced motion for animated surfaces and keep dense technical views keyboard-accessible and readable at common laptop and desktop sizes.
